The Architect Track: Designing EMA's Future

The Architect is a pinnacle IC role, blending deep technical expertise with business acumen to guide our long-term technical strategy, especially as we modernize with AWS.

Core Responsibilities

  • Design and guide complex, large-scale software systems.
  • Ensure solutions are scalable, resilient, and secure in the cloud.
  • Align technical decisions with EMA's long-term business goals.
  • Mentor senior engineers and set technical standards.

Key Skills for Success

  • Technical Mastery: Deep expertise in cloud platforms (AWS), system design, and software patterns.
  • Business Acumen: Understanding of business goals, ROI, and market factors to make strategic technical tradeoffs.
  • Leadership & Communication: Ability to influence without authority, articulate complex ideas clearly, and lead technical teams.

Specialized Paths: QA & DevOps

QA and DevOps are foundational, strategic disciplines vital to our success. Explore the dedicated career ladders for these mission-critical roles.

The Quality Assurance Path

The QA role has evolved from a gatekeeper to a proactive strategist. This path focuses on ensuring quality from the very start of the development lifecycle.

Junior QA Engineer

Executes test cases, identifies bugs, and masters testing tools and procedures.

Mid-Level QA Engineer

Independently designs test plans, implements automation scripts, and collaborates with developers.

Senior QA Engineer

Leads project testing efforts, designs complex test strategies, and mentors junior team members.

QA Manager / Director

Provides strategic direction for the entire quality department and manages the QA team and processes.

The DevOps & SRE Path

This path applies software engineering principles to operations, building and running the large-scale, resilient systems that power our healthtech platform.

DevOps vs. SRE

DevOps is a culture focused on automating and integrating development and IT operations to increase speed and quality. SRE (Site Reliability Engineering) is a discipline that uses software engineering to ensure system reliability and availability.

DevOps Engineer

Responsible for the end-to-end application lifecycle, building CI/CD pipelines, automating infrastructure, and fostering a "you build it, you run it" culture.

Site Reliability Engineer (SRE)

Focuses on the stability and reliability of the production environment, developing software for operations, managing incidents, and setting service-level objectives (SLOs).

Practices that Drive Progress

CI/CD & Shift-Left Testing

Automating builds, testing, and deployments frees you from manual tasks to focus on high-impact problem-solving. Shift-left testing makes quality a shared, proactive responsibility, not an afterthought.

Cloud-Native Development

Our move to AWS opens doors to new roles like Cloud Architect and Cloud-Native Engineer. Mastering containers, microservices, and Infrastructure as Code is key to future growth.
